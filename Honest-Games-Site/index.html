Decpetive Patterns Defined

Deceptive patterns (also known as dark patterns) are design choices in websites, apps, and video games used to intentionally manipulate users into taking actions they otherwise would not. From loot boxes to time-limited sales, deceptive patterns in video games actively push users to spend more time and money in the game.

Documented Decpetive Patterns in Video Games

Loot Boxes

Loot boxes are psychology-akin to gambling and have no age restrictions in the United States. They are similar to baseball cards as loot boxes contain random rewards that have random rarities.

Learn More

Pay to Win

Ability to gain in-game progress by spending money, usually gaining advantages over other players. Games can manipulate players by slowly ramping up the difficulty and reduce the effectiveness of the player, pushing payment as the only way of progression.

Learn More

Impersonation

Some games will impersonate your real life friends using your contact data, trying to trick you into thinking your friends are playing. Sometimes these "friends" will have fake high-scores or send you in-game gifts.
